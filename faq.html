The minimum age to rent a car varies by rental car company and location. In most cases, you must be at least 21 years old to rent a car. Some rental agencies may require you to be 25 or older. Younger drivers (under 25) may be subject to a "young driver surcharge.

ypically, you will need the following documents to rent a car: A valid driver's license: You must have a current, unexpired driver's license. A major credit card: Rental car agencies usually require a credit card for payment and security deposit purposes. Proof of insurance: Your personal auto insurance policy or a separate rental car insurance policy may be required. Check with the rental agency for specific requirements.

While many rental car companies prefer customers to have a credit card, some may accept a debit card or cash deposit. However, this varies by location and rental agency. Be sure to check the specific payment policies of the rental company you plan to use.

Rental cars often come with basic insurance coverage, such as Collision Damage Waiver (CDW) and Liability Insurance. However, the coverage may not be comprehensive. You may have the option to purchase additional insurance for added protection. Check with the rental company to understand the insurance options available and decide what's best for your needs.
